Essential Maintenance Tasks

Regular maintenance prevents breakdowns and extends your car's life. Here are the most important tasks and how often to do them.

Tire Pressure Check

How often: Monthly, and before long trips.

Why: Underinflated tires reduce fuel economy, wear unevenly, and can blow out. Overinflated tires have less grip and a harsher ride.

How: Use a tire pressure gauge ($5 at any auto store). Recommended PSI is on a sticker inside the driver's door or in the manual. Fill at a gas station if needed.

How often: Every 12,000â€“15,000 miles (or yearly).

Why: The engine air filter keeps dirt out of the engine. A clogged filter reduces performance and fuel economy.

Coolant/Antifreeze Check

How often: Check level monthly. Flush and replace every 30,000 miles or 2â€“5 years.

Why: Coolant prevents the engine from overheating in summer and freezing in winter. Low coolant can cause catastrophic engine damage.

How: Check the translucent reservoir under the hood. The level should be between MIN and MAX. Top off if low (use 50/50 mix or pre-mixed coolant).

Brake Inspection

How often: Every 10,000 miles or at every other oil change. Replace pads when they get thin.

Why: Brakes are your #1 safety feature. Worn pads reduce stopping power and can damage rotors (much more expensive).

Signs of wear: Squealing, grinding noise, or the car pulling to one side when braking. Get them checked immediately.

Battery Check

How often: Test yearly, especially before winter. Replace every 3â€“5 years.

Why: A weak battery leaves you stranded. Cold weather kills old batteries fast.

How: Most auto parts stores test batteries for free. Look for corrosion (white/blue crust) on terminals and clean it off with baking soda and water.

Check All Lights

How often: Monthly.

Why: Broken lights reduce visibility and can get you pulled over.

How: Walk around the car at night and make sure headlights, taillights, brake lights, turn signals, and reverse lights all work. Replacing a bulb is usually easy (YouTube your car model + "replace headlight").

Keep It Clean

How often: Wash every 2 weeks. Wax every 3â€“6 months.

Why: Dirt, salt, and bird droppings damage paint over time. Wax protects the clear coat and keeps the car looking new.

Bonus: Vacuum the interior monthly. A clean car is easier to sell later.
