How to Change a Flat Tire

  1. Safety first: Pull over to a safe, flat area away from traffic. Turn on hazard lights.
  2. Engage parking brake and place wheel chocks (or heavy rocks) behind the tires.
  3. Get your spare tire, jack, and lug wrench from the trunk.
  4. Loosen the lug nuts on the flat tire (don't remove yet) while the car is still on the ground.
  5. Position the jack under the car's jack point (check your manual) and raise the car until the tire is off the ground.
  6. Remove the lug nuts completely and take off the flat tire.
  7. Mount the spare tire, hand-tighten the lug nuts.
  8. Lower the car and tighten lug nuts in a star pattern.
  9. Drive slowly to a repair shop‚Äîspare tires are temporary!

‚ö†Ô∏è Never go under a car supported only by a jack. Don't drive fast on a spare tire.

How to Jump-Start a Dead Battery

  1. Park a working car close to yours, but don't let them touch.
  2. Turn both cars off. Open both hoods.
  3. Attach one red clamp to the dead battery's positive (+) terminal.
  4. Attach the other red clamp to the working battery's positive (+) terminal.
  5. Attach one black clamp to the working battery's negative (‚àí) terminal.
  6. Attach the other black clamp to an unpainted metal surface on the dead car's engine (not the battery).
  7. Start the working car and let it run for 2‚Äì3 minutes.
  8. Try to start the dead car. If it starts, let both run for 5 minutes.
  9. Remove cables in reverse order: black from dead car, black from working car, red from working car, red from dead car.
  10. Drive the revived car for at least 20 minutes to recharge the battery.

‚ö†Ô∏è Never let the red and black clamps touch each other. Sparks can cause an explosion.

What to Do If Your Car Overheats

  1. Turn off the A/C immediately.
  2. Turn the heater to maximum heat with the fan on high (this pulls heat away from the engine).
  3. Pull over to a safe spot as soon as possible. Turn off the engine.
  4. Wait at least 15‚Äë20 minutes before opening the hood.
  5. Check the coolant reservoir level (never open the radiator cap when hot).
  6. If coolant is low and you have extra, add it to the reservoir once the engine cools.
  7. If it overheats again after adding coolant, call a tow truck. Don't keep driving.

‚ö†Ô∏è Never open a hot radiator cap‚Äîboiling coolant can spray out and cause severe burns.

Car Won't Start? Troubleshooting

Try these in order:

  1. Check if the battery is dead: Lights dim or don't come on? Try jump-starting.
  2. Make sure the car is in Park (automatic) or Neutral (manual). Some cars won't start in gear.
  3. Check for a clicking sound: Click-click-click usually means a dead battery or bad starter.
  4. Check the fuel gauge: Out of gas? It happens more than you'd think.
  5. Try turning the steering wheel: If the wheel is locked, the ignition might not turn. Wiggle the wheel while turning the key.
  6. Check if the key fob battery is dead (for push-button start cars). Hold the fob close to the start button.
  7. If none of this works, call roadside assistance or a tow truck.

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't keep cranking the starter for more than 10 seconds at a time‚Äîyou can damage it.

Locked Out of Your Car

Stay calm and try these options:

  1. Check all doors and the trunk. Sometimes one door is unlocked or the trunk has a release to the back seat.
  2. Look for a spare key. Check your wallet, magnetic key holder under the car, or ask family if they have one.
  3. Call roadside assistance if you have AAA, insurance roadside help, or manufacturer assistance (like OnStar).
  4. Use your phone app if your car has remote unlock via smartphone (many newer models do).
  5. Call a locksmith. Get quotes from 2‚Äì3 locksmiths. Average cost is $50‚Äì$150.
  6. Last resort: Call the dealer. They can make a new key, but it's expensive ($200‚Äì$500) and may take time.

‚ö†Ô∏è Never try to break a window yourself or use wire hangers‚Äîyou can damage the door or trigger alarms. Always call a professional.

Stuck in Snow or Mud

Get unstuck safely:

  1. Don't floor the gas pedal. Spinning tires dig you in deeper and can overheat the transmission.
  2. Clear snow/mud from around the tires. Use a shovel, stick, or your hands (wear gloves).
  3. Rock the car back and forth. Shift between Drive and Reverse gently, using momentum to break free.
  4. Add traction under the tires. Use floor mats, cardboard, sand, kitty litter, or branches.
  5. Let air out of tires slightly (to about 20 PSI) for more surface contact. Re-inflate at a gas station immediately after.
  6. Get help pushing if safe. Have someone push while you gently accelerate.
  7. Call a tow truck if you're really stuck or in a dangerous location.

‚ö†Ô∏è Never stand directly in front or behind a stuck car‚Äîit can lurch forward/backward suddenly. Keep exhaust pipe clear to avoid carbon monoxide buildup.
